Welcome to the Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the world's largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization. Located near the Giza Pyramids, it houses over 100,000 artifacts from ancient Egypt, including the complete Tutankhamun collection displayed together for the first time.

Ancient Egyptian Civilization

Ancient Egypt, one of the world's greatest civilizations, flourished for over 3,000 years along the Nile River. From the construction of the magnificent pyramids to the development of hieroglyphic writing, the ancient Egyptians made remarkable contributions to human history.

  • Pharaohs & Dynasties: Powerful rulers who were considered living gods
  • Pyramids & Temples: Architectural marvels that still stand today
  • Mummification: Advanced preservation techniques for the afterlife
  • Hieroglyphics: One of the earliest writing systems

Featured Collections

Our museum showcases the treasures of ancient Egypt across multiple eras:

  • Tutankhamun Gallery: The complete collection of the boy king
  • Royal Mummies: Preserved remains of Egypt's greatest pharaohs
  • Statues & Sculptures: Monumental works from temples and tombs
  • Daily Life Artifacts: Tools, jewelry, and household items
  • Papyrus Scrolls: Ancient texts and religious documents
